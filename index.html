Who was Bob Marley?

Bob Marley was a Jamaican singer, songwriter and guitar player, responsible for making reggae a rhythm known worldwide. He was also one of the greatest representatives of the Rastafarian religious movement.

Classic picture of singer Bob Marley with smile on his face.

Bob Marley Biography:

  • Robert Nesta Marley was born in Saint Ann, rural area in northern Jamaica, on February 6, 1945.He was the son of a white military man, a captain in the English army, and a young Jamaican black woman(Cedella Booker ).

  • When his father died, in 1955, Marley and his mother went to live in the community of Kingston, where he suffered discrimination for being mulatto, a fact that was not well regarded by blacks of that time and place.

  • After his dad died, Cedella Booker married Toddy Livingstone who had a son called Bunny Wailer, who lived with Bob and became his best friend.

Musical Career:

  • Bob Marley and Bunny Wailers, started to making songs with their friend Peter McIntosh, who joined in the group. Then, in 1962, the businessman Leslie Kong heard Marley sing and invited him to record in the studio the single “Judge Not”, it was the beginning of Marley's career.

  • In the next year, Marley formed, along with Bunny and Peter, a reggae group called "Wailing Waillers". They played the country's main rhythm, where reggae originated, which is based on a mix of African sounds with rhythm & blues (R&B).

  • The band's first single, “Simmer Down”, was the most performed song on Jamaican radio for two straight months. At the time, the group already had three more members: Junior Braithwaite and, the backing-vocals Beverly Kelso and Cherry Smith.

  • In 1966, Bob Marley married Rita Anderson and traveled to the United States, where he stayed for eight months with his mother and stepfather.

The Wailer:

  • When Bob returned to Jamaica, he met his old friends Bunny and Peter, and return the group that changed its name to "The Wailers".

  • Between the years of 1970 and 1976, they had great success with new participants in the group, such as bassist Aston Barret and drummer Carton Barret.Also with the album "Burnin", Bob and Peter composed, "Get Up, Stand Up" and "I Shot the Sheriff", which was recorded by Eric Clapton, in 1974, and became hit number one in the United States.

  • In 1976, the group released their fourth studio album, "Rastaman Vibrations". At that time, the band started to be recognized as "Bob Marley & The Wailers". Soon, the album reaches the top positions of the music charts in the United States.

War in Jamaica:

  • At that time, Jamaica was going through a serious political and social crisis. The musician, with great prestige, decided to do a free concert at the National Heroes Park in Kingston, with the aim of calling for an end to the conflicts between the various gangs.

  • On December 3, 1976, two days before the concert, “Smile Jamaica”, organized by Prime Minister Michael Manley, Bob Marley was attacked when armed men entered his home on Hope Road. The shots seriously injured his wife Rita Marley and his manager Dom Taylor, while Marley suffered minor chest and arm injuries. Despite what happened, even though injured, Marley went on stage and performed for a crowd of 80,000 people.

  • After the incident, Marley decided to move to London. In 1977 he recorded the album "Exodos", which remained for more than 50 weeks in the top positions the most played musics in England. The track “One Love” was a huge success.

  • Back in Jamaica, Marley organizes the “One Love Peace Concert”, where the big moment was the handshake, given on stage, between The Prime Minister Michael Manley and his rival Edward Seaga. Consequently, Bob Marley received the "Peace Medal" at the United Nations headquarters in New York. Still in 1977, Marley embarked for Ethiopia, the African country where Rastafari originated.

  • Bob Marley's music was important for the acceptance of Reggae in the world, which became one of the most popular rythms. Nowadays, Marley is considered a legend, as he disseminated his ideas of peace through music.

Death:

  • In 1977 he was diagnosed with aggressive skin cancer and refused to treat it for religious reasons.

  • Bob Marley died in Miami, on May 11, 1981, victim of cancer. His funeral was honored by the head of state and his date of birth is a national holiday in Jamaica.
